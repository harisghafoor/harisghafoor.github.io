Research Focus

Exploring computational methods for medical imaging and inverse problems

Research Overview

As an MS-PhD student in Computational Science and Engineering at Yonsei University, my research is focused on developing advanced computational methods for medical imaging applications, with a particular emphasis on ultrasound synthesis and inverse problem modeling.

My work combines elements of signal processing, machine learning, and computational mathematics to address complex challenges in medical diagnostics and imaging technologies.

Main Research Areas

Inverse Learning Problem Modeling

Inverse problems involve determining unknown causes based on observations of their effects. In medical imaging, this often means reconstructing images from sensor data. My research focuses on:

  • Developing novel mathematical frameworks for ill-posed inverse problems
  • Creating regularization methods for stable and accurate solutions
  • Implementing machine learning approaches to improve traditional inverse problem solvers
  • Applying these methods to real-world medical imaging challenges

Ultrasound Synthesis Methods

Ultrasound imaging is widely used due to its safety, affordability, and real-time capabilities. My research in this area includes:

  • Creating generative models for synthetic ultrasound image production
  • Developing physics-informed neural networks that incorporate acoustic propagation principles
  • Improving training data for AI-powered ultrasound diagnostics
  • Bridging the gap between simulated and real ultrasound data

Computational Methods for Medical Imaging

Modern medical imaging requires sophisticated computational techniques for image formation, enhancement, and analysis. My work includes:

  • Developing efficient algorithms for medical image reconstruction
  • Creating deep learning architectures specifically tailored for medical imaging applications
  • Implementing methods for noise reduction and artifact removal
  • Designing real-time processing techniques for clinical applications

Current Research Projects

Advanced Ultrasound Synthesis using Generative Adversarial Networks

This project explores the application of GANs to generate realistic ultrasound images for training medical imaging AI systems. By creating synthetic but physiologically accurate images, we can improve the training data available for diagnostic algorithms while preserving patient privacy.

In Progress 2023-Present

Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Inverse Problems in Medical Imaging

This research integrates physical laws and constraints into neural network architectures to solve inverse problems in medical imaging. By incorporating domain knowledge from physics, these networks can provide more accurate and physically plausible solutions compared to traditional approaches.

In Progress 2023-Present

Time Series Analysis and Forecasting in Medical Data

Building on my previous work with LSTM Encoded NBEATS models, this project applies advanced time series modeling techniques to medical data. The goal is to develop predictive models for patient monitoring and diagnostics that can capture complex temporal patterns in physiological signals.

Planned 2024

Laboratory and Research Group

I am conducting my research at the Computational Medical Imaging Laboratory at Yonsei University, where we focus on developing advanced computational methods for various medical imaging modalities. Our interdisciplinary team brings together expertise in computer science, electrical engineering, physics, and medicine.

Our laboratory is equipped with state-of-the-art computing resources, including high-performance GPU clusters for deep learning research, and maintains close collaborations with medical institutions to ensure our research has practical clinical applications.
